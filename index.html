visualization by : Tan Jia Wen

Introduction

Gender equality, also known as sexual equality or equality of the sexes, is the state of
equal ease of access to resources and opportunities regardless of gender, including
economic participation and decision-making; and the state of valuing different behaviors,
aspirations and needs equally, regardless of gender. (Wikipedia)

Gender Equality Over Various Regions

Different parts of the world are home to various cultures and ethnicities and thus also
house different perspectives on equality among the genders. The following map
visualization (right) shows the global ranking of each country's equality throughout the years.
This is to provide insight on which countries have the most equal opportunities amongst
other countries in the region and to view how each country improves or neglects gender
equality within their borders.

Country Rankings Over The Years

For those seeking countries with equal opportunities, it is important to take note of how a
specific country ranks over time, as this is a clear indicator as to whether or not gender
equality is the forefront of the country's concerns. The bump chart shows how the rankings
of the 10 most gender equal countries in 2006 change from the years 2006 to 2013. To
further assist with comparisons between countries, the option to filter countries is present,
this can be done by shift-clicking multiple countries from the legend.

The top 10 most gender equal countries in 2006 include Denmark, Finland, Germany,
Iceland, Ireland, New Zealand, Norway, Philippines, Sweden, United Kingdom.

Country Scores Over The Years

Similar to the reasoning of the bump chart provided above, the comparisons of scores
would also provide the same information but with deeper insight, it has the added benefit
of not falling or rising in relation to the scores of other countries. A comparison utilizing
scores instead of ranks would be more objective despite the similarity. The provided Line Chart
shows how the overall score's of the top 30 countries regarding gender equality change
over the years, to assist with comparison and the option to filter countries is present, this can be
done by shift-clicking multiple countries from the legend.

Similar to the line chart, the heatmap shows how scores for the top 30 countries change over the years, the main advantage of the heatmap over the line graph is to compare the scores between all countries at once. For example the visualization shows that the countries with the highest equality with least variability over the years include Sweden, Iceland, Norway and Finland.

Correlation Of Equality In Various Aspects In A Country

Gender equality is often measured on a more precise and refined scale. In essence, gender equality within a country can be subdivided into various categories, some of these subcategories include Economic participation and opportunities, Educational Attainment, Political Empowerment, and Healthcare equality. The scatter plot highlights how these 4 attributes correlate to one another, since all correlation lines point up and leftward, all 4 have a positive correlation with one another. Due to the concentration of the points, the distribution may not be clear, as such a zoom feature is provided which can be used by scrolling the mouse wheel while hovering over a scatter plot, along with this translation is also provided to further explore the distribution which can be accessed by clicking and dragging the cursor
